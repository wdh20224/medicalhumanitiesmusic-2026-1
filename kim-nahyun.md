# Keys to the Heart

In *Keys to the Heart* Jo-ha, a former boxer who was once a champion but is now nowhere to go, reunites with his mother, who accidentally broke up after 17 years, and faces his unexpected younger brother, Jin-tae. Facing Jin-tae, a servant syndrome who has a genius for piano, he learns the meaning of brotherhood and family again. It deals with Jin-tae's Servant syndrome and conflict and growth over piano talent. Composer = Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky Pyotr – Piano Concerto No. 1 in B-flat minor, Op. 23. Tchaikovsky's Concerto No. 1 requires grand, difficult, and splendid technique, and Jin-tae, who is a patient with Servant syndrome and was not good at communicating in everyday life, shows perfect control and explosive performance on the keyboard provides an intense catharsis to the audience. In the film, music is both a channel for Jin-tae to communicate with the world and a device to expand his inner world, which is trapped in the framework of disability. In the work, music acts as a medium to sublimate Jin-tae's disability into a special language, not a deficiency.
